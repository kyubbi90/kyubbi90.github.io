Utilizing SEO Best Practices

Eliminating duplicate content so your site doesn’t get penalized

  • Duplicate Content should be avoided to prevent confusion.
  • Use unique titles for pages
  • Canonical tag tells a search engine where the content came from originally

Avoiding unnatural keyword usage

  • Write naturally instead of densely on your site.
  • Cloaking - hiding text in a site
  • Cloaking causes penalization

Using rich media to promote your SEO results

  • Compress jpeg to reduce load.
  • Provide summary of video onsite.
  • Give options to share video

Minimizing your website loading speeds

  • Site load speed is not ranked by the google algorithm.
  • Use a quality hosting service and minimize code - to reduce load speed.

Correlating your SEO efforts to sales or other quantitative measures

  • Measurable goals - Product purchase, social interactions, and product reviews.
  • Use Google analytics to set site goals

Implementing On-Page Optimization

Aligning your domain name with your target audience

  • Find site name including keyword
  • Avoid .cc, .info, and .biz
  • A short domain name is preferred.

Design your landing page to drive deeper engagement

  • Use CTA to have users deeper explore a site.
  • Keep content on homepage light
  • Match site design with consumer

Optimizing your product pages via breadcrumbs, page titles, and social links

  • Breadcrumb show user where they are now
  • Social sharing buttons uses link building
  • Use unique product descriptions.

Understanding how mobile usability affects SEO

  • Sites without mobile-friendly badge may have not be ranked high on mobile.
  • Test with google’s mobile test tool
  • Design with mobile users in mind

Incorporating Measurement and Optimization Tools

Using Optimizely for A/B testing

  • Split-test - Display slightly different versions to users
  • Optimizely can be used to compare to display possible changes.
  • Testing is used through optimizely code

Harnessing Google Analytics to better understand site visitors

  • Google tracks demographics including countries, browsers and languages
  • Keywords used to find site through searches are tracked

Employing heat mapping tools to measure and monitor page usage

  • Heat Mapping tracks aspects of a site to find where users are clicking.
  • Scroll map shows how far users are viewing on a page.

Applying Moz for larger scale sites

  • MOZ gives in depth interview on sites searches and keywords.
  • On-page grader grades pages use of keyword.
