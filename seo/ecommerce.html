WordPress Ecommerce Core Concepts

The following notes were taken by from watching this Lynda.com course.

Understanding Your Market

Choosing a product to sell

  • Ecommerce used to be expensive to set up
  • 1. Choose and interest - hobby or other
  • 2. Choose a product - physical or digital

Defining your audience

  • Look from customers point of view
  • Create personas of customer type
  • If catering towards beginners give more info compared to advanced customers

Organizing your products

  • Bundles, add-ons, subscriptions are types of ways to sell products
  • If customers have to research the product elsewhere there is no guarantee they’ll return to your site

Offering pre-sales to validate your product

  • Try selling one product before selling multiple types
  • Crowdfunding can be used to gauge interest in a product or service

Marketing Your Site

Using marketing to increase site traffic

  • Gaining traffic requires time or money
  • Advertising gets quick results with many different options to use
  • Social media - can reach people who aren't searching

Understanding your customer lifetime value(CLV)

  • CLV - How much profit to make off one customer
  • CoA - cost per visit divided by conversion rate

Increasing CLV with repeat customers

  • Encourage customers to return
  • Consumable products have a lot of opportunity to return
  • Social media and newsletters are good ways of building relationships with customers

Increasing your average order value

  • Premium version of products such as bundles are good ways of getting customers to spend more on a single visit
  • Use premium products to make other products prices look more enticing by being cheaper
  • Cross-selling - giving recommended related products

Selling Products

Getting paid

  • Things needed to get paid online: Website, payment gateway, merchant account, bank account
  • Payment gateway checks if enough money is available for transaction
  • Modern gateway has merchant account included and is recommended for beginners

Examining your needs

  • What payments do you want to accept
  • PayPal is safer than credit cards and is included in WordPress Plugins
  • Amazon is also available
  • Where will you accept currency from?

Checking out with PayPal

  • PayPal is an offsite gateway that doesn't require a paypal account
  • PayPal standard is free

Checking out with Stripe

  • Payment gateway- stripe
  • Uses credit and debit cards for transaction

Shipping, Orders, and Taxes

Calculating shipping costs

  • Free shipping, flat rate, and table of shipping rates
  • Free shipping encourages users to spend more for free shipping costs

Managing orders

  • Create roles to manage orders as complete or other
  • Orders can be refunded for a number of reasons

Managing inventory and listings across platforms

  • Good idea to sell from own site and sites like amazon
  • Sites like tradegecko can sync data between these sites

Understanding taxes

  • Different forms of tax- Federal, state, county, city
  • Consult a tax professional for info
  • In US show tax exclusive prices- show tax separate from prices

Collecting EU value-added tax(VAT)

  • If someone from the EU purchases a digital good from you, you must pay that country’s taxes
  • Taxamo calculates tax pricing for you

Securing Your Site

Understanding the basics of Secure Sockets Layer(SSL)

  • Security importance - can be held financially liable, Lose customers trust
  • Unsecured traffic can be interrupted
  • All sensitive info should be secured
  • HTTPS should be visible to show security

Protecting sensitive data

  • Force users to use Https
  • WordPress can be used force secure checkout
  • Protect admin login page

Using WordPress for Ecommerce

Using WordPress platforms

  • Plugins are open source
  • Some are freemium and require payment for advanced options

Implementing third-party platforms

  • Hosted platforms are easier to set up and harder to customize
  • Gumroad supports selling products for free
  • Gumroad charges higher rates than stripe

Testing and Optimization

Testing your site

  • Test checkout process with separate carts
  • Test each product and shipping carts
  • Test payment gateways

Optimizing your site

  • Always room to improve
  • Install analytics to analyze how site is used
  • Hotjar can record a users window to watch how users use entire site
